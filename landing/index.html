The Basics

What is a GI Tag? Explained in 2 Minutes.

A Geographical Indication tag — GI tag — is India's official government stamp that says: this product can ONLY come from this specific place, made by the people of that region, using their traditional methods. No imitation allowed. By law.

World Example Indian GI Example What It Means
Champagne can only come from Champagne, France. Everything else is sparkling wine. Kashmir Saffron can only come from Pampore, Kashmir. The geography makes the product. No other place can produce the same thing.
Parmesan can only come from Parma, Italy — by law. Lakdong Turmeric can only come from Meghalaya's Jaintia Hills. The flavour, nutrition, and quality come from the soil itself.
Scotch Whisky must be distilled and aged in Scotland. Govind Bhog Rice can only come from West Bengal's Hooghly district. When you buy it with a GI tag, you are buying the real thing. Always.
